Discover the unique ingredients and bold flavors that define Marinduqueño cuisine.

I. The "Crayfish" of Marinduque: Manakla

The most prized crustacean in Marinduque is the Manakla, a snapping shrimp found in the island's vast mangroves.

Snapping Shrimp Mangrove Habitat Local Delicacy
  • Taste Profile: Sweet like a prawn, yet as firm as crab meat.
  • Signature Prep: Ginataang Manakla – slow-cooked in thick coconut milk with ginger and local chili.

Local tip: Best paired with steaming white rice and a view of the sea.

II. Kari-Kari

The Island’s Dark Stew

Forget the peanut sauce—in Marinduque, Kari-Kari is a bold, savory blood stew similar to dinuguan but with a richer, more concentrated texture.

  • Rich with pig’s blood and internal organs.
  • Served thick, almost dry, for maximum flavor.
  • A staple during the town fiestas of Santa Cruz.

III. Adobo sa Ilong

Yellow Adobo

This vibrant version replaces soy sauce with turmeric (dilaw), resulting in a bright, aromatic dish that feels like sunshine on a plate.

  • Deeply garlicky and earthily aromatic.
  • Rich, oily finish typical of island cooking.
  • Distinctly "Dilaw" (Yellow) due to fresh turmeric.
