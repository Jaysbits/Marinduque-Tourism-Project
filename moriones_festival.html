I. What is the Moriones Festival?

Held annually during Holy Week, this world-renowned festival is a week-long celebration where locals dress as Roman centurions in a grand display of faith and folk theater.

🎭 The Name

Derived from "Morion", the Spanish term for the open-faced helmet worn by 16th-century soldiers.

👥 The Vow

Participants consider this a "Panata" (religious vow), enduring the tropical heat as an act of penance.

II. The Legend of Saint Longinus

The festival dramatizes the story of Longinus, the half-blind Roman centurion who pierced the side of Jesus on the cross. According to tradition, a drop of Christ's blood fell into his eye, miraculously restoring his sight.

Note: The festival culminates in the "Pugutan," a mock execution of Longinus for his newfound faith.

III. The Mask: Folk Art Masterpiece

Morion masks are hand-carved from Dapdap or Santing wood. They feature fierce, intimidating expressions, prominent noses, and are topped with ornate headgear called penacho.

Wearing the heavy wooden mask under the sun is the central sacrifice of the participant's religious commitment.

💡 Visitor Pro-Tips

  • Main Hubs: The best places to witness the festival are Boac, Gasan, and Mogpog.
  • Interaction: Watch out for the Antipo—mischievous Moriones who play jokes on the crowd!
  • Respect the Vow: Remember that for many, this is a religious duty. Please ask before taking close-up flash photos during processions.
