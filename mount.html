I. Mt. Malindig – The Guardian Stratovolcano

Rising 1,157 meters above sea level, Mt. Malindig is the highest peak in Marinduque. This potentially active volcano stands as a lush sentinel between Buenavista and Torrijos.

Elevation: 1,157m Status: Potentially Active

🌋

The leaning silhouette that inspired the name "Marinduque."

🥾 II. The Hike (2026)

  • Difficulty: Level 3/9 (Moderate)
  • Trail: Established farm trails passing through coconut plantations.
  • The Peak: Offers 360° views of the Sibuyan Sea and Tres Reyes Islands.

🦅 IV. Biodiversity Spotlight

  • Mossy Forest: Lush upper elevations with rare orchids and ferns.
  • Wildlife: Habitat of the Philippine Brown Deer and Serpent Eagle.
  • Protection: Declared a Strict Protection Zone as of 2026.

III. Recovery at Malbog Sulfur Hot Springs

After the demanding hike, soothe your muscles at the mountain’s base. These natural volcanic springs are famous for their medicinal properties.

  • Healing: High sulfur content believed to aid skin and muscle recovery.
  • Experience: Choose between public dipping pools or private resort facilities.
  • Nature: Authentic "rotten egg" scent from the active volcanic vents.
