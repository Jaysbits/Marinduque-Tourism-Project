I. The Gold of the Earth: Uraro

Uraro (Arrowroot) cookies are Marinduque’s most famous delicacy, known for their melt-in-your-mouth texture and powdery finish.

  • Source: Made from arrowroot starch grown in Gasan and Boac
  • Process: Traditional extraction through grinding, soaking, and sun-drying
  • Health Factor: Naturally gluten-free and easy to digest

II. The “Lalaki” Bibingka

Unlike typical bibingka, Boac’s Bibingkang Lalaki uses coconut wine (tuba) instead of yeast.

  • Unique Feature: Slightly sour flavor from fermentation
  • Texture: Dense and hearty
  • Flavor: Balanced sweetness from muscovado sugar

III. Pan de San Nicolas

These traditional cookies are often found in heritage homes and are stamped with the image of St. Nicholas.

  • Design: Intricate religious patterns
  • Belief: Considered to have healing or protective properties
  • Use: Sometimes kept as keepsakes or talismans
