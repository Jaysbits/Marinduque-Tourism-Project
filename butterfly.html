I. The Butterfly Capital – A Winged Industry

Marinduque produces around 80% of the Philippines' butterfly exports, making it the "Butterfly Capital" of the country. What began as a small hobby in the 1970s has grown into a thriving industry centered in Boac and Gasan.

  • Export: Pupae are shipped worldwide to butterfly houses
  • Tradition: Butterflies are released in celebrations for good luck

II. The Life Cycle: From Farm to Flight

  • 1. Breeding: Raised in enclosures with host plants
  • 2. Egg Collection: Farmers gather tiny eggs
  • 3. Larval Stage: Caterpillars grow rapidly
  • 4. Pupa Stage: Chrysalis exported for hatching abroad

III. Iconic Species of Marinduque

  • Paper Kite (Idea leuconoe): Large white butterfly with slow flight
  • Common Mormon (Papilio polytes): Known for mimicry
  • Clipper (Parthenos sylvia): Fast flyer with vibrant colors

IV. The Bila-Bila Festival

  • 🦋 Parade: Locals wear large butterfly wings
  • ✨ Theme: Represents transformation and resilience

V. Conservation & Eco-Tourism

  • Eco-Sanctuaries: Farms now serve as conservation sites
  • Research: Used by biology students
  • Tourism: Visitors can interact with live butterflies
