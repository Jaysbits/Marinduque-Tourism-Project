I. The Anatomy of a Morion Mask

A true Marinduqueño Morion mask is a handcrafted masterpiece, often carved by the wearer themselves.

  • 🌳 The Wood: Carved from Dapdap or Santing—lightweight for the wearer.
  • 👺 The Features: Fierce eyes and prominent noses symbolize Roman authority.
  • 🧔 The Beard: Traditionally made from real horsehair or abaca fibers.
  • 🌸 The Penacho: A decorative crest of bright flowers or feathers.

II. The Mask-Making Process

1. Selection & Drying

Wood is harvested and dried for months to prevent cracking during carving.

2. Rough Carving

Artisans use a batikan (chisel) to block out the facial structure.

3. Fine Refining

Details like the wrinkles, eyelids, and helmet patterns are meticulously carved.

4. Painting

Finished with bright enamel paints (red, gold, and flesh tones) for a glossy look.

III. The "Panata" (Sacred Vow)

"We don't just wear the mask for the tourists. We wear it as a form of sacrifice. The heat, the weight, and the difficulty are our way of asking forgiveness or giving thanks."

Rule of Silence: Moriones traditionally remain silent while masked to maintain their penance.
Anonymity: Most wearers keep their identity hidden until Easter Sunday.
