I. Heritage Houses – Echoes of the Spanish Era

Marinduque is one of the few places in the Philippines where history is still lived daily. The towns of Boac and Gasan are filled with Bahay na Bato houses that have stood for centuries.

Boac Heritage District

Location: Mataas na Bayan

  • 🏛️ Casa de Don Emilio: A restored landmark turned boutique hotel.
  • 🪟 Capiz Windows: Classic shell panes and Narra hardwood floors.
  • 🌬️ Ventanillas: Small shutters beneath windows to invite river breezes.

Gasan’s “Ancestral Row”

Location: Poblacion Gasan

  • 🎨 Artistic Flair: Famous for houses painted in vibrant pastel colors.
  • 🎭 Balconies: Featuring ornate ironwork, perfect for viewing the Gasang-Gasang festival.
  • 📸 Photography: A favorite spot for heritage enthusiasts.

II. Anatomy of a Bahay na Bato

Zaguan

Ground floor for kalesas and harvest storage.

Caida

Main hallway used for receiving guests.

Volada

Flying gallery for servants and airflow.

Calado

Intricate wood carvings for air circulation.

III. Preservation in 2026

  • 📌 Adopt-a-House: Joint restoration program by the NHCP.
  • ☕ Adaptive Reuse: Many homes now serve as cozy cafés and galleries.
  • 🚶 Heritage Walk: Join the guided tours starting from Boac Plaza daily.

Join the movement to save our history!

📸 Explorer Tips

  • Be Respectful: Many of these are private residences. Always ask before taking photos of interiors.
  • Lighting: 7:00–9:00 AM provides the softest light for capturing textures.
  • Look Up: Don't miss the Calado (wood carvings) near the roofline!
