I. Mt. Malindig – The Guardian Stratovolcano

Mount Malindig is the highest peak in Marinduque, rising 1,157 meters above sea level. Located between Buenavista and Torrijos, it is a potentially active volcano and a favorite destination for hikers and nature lovers.

  • The Silhouette: Its "leaning" shape inspired the island’s name
  • The Mossy Forest: Higher elevations feature lush moss, orchids, and ferns

II. The Hike: Scaling the Peak (2026 Guide)

  • Difficulty: Moderate (Level 3/9)
  • Trail: Well-established and used by local farmers
  • View: 360° view of Sibuyan Sea, Tres Reyes Islands, and distant mountains
  • Radio Station Trail: Popular stopping point with stunning views

III. Recovery at Malbog Sulfur Hot Springs

After the hike, visitors relax at Malbog Hot Springs located at the mountain’s base.

  • Healing Waters: Rich in sulfur, believed to soothe muscles and skin
  • The Scent: Natural sulfur smell ("rotten egg")
  • The Pools: Public pools and private resorts available

IV. Biodiversity Spotlight

  • Protected Zone: Upper slopes declared a Strict Protection Zone (2026)
  • Wildlife: Philippine Brown Deer and Narrow-mouthed Frog sightings
  • Bird Watching: Philippine Serpent Eagle near the peak
