I. Marinduque National Museum – Treasures of the Deep

The museum is housed in a restored 19th-century Spanish building in Boac, once used as the Presidencia (seat of government) and even a provincial jail. Its thick stone walls and wooden beams reflect the island’s colonial past.

II. The Shipwreck Gallery

Marinduque’s surrounding waters were once part of the ancient maritime trade routes known as the "Silk Road of the Sea."

  • Gaspar Island Shipwreck: Discovered in the 1980s with Ming Dynasty porcelain
  • Artifacts: Celadon plates, stoneware jars, and blue-and-white ceramics

III. Archaeological Wonders

  • Burial Jars: Used for pre-colonial burial traditions
  • Stone Tools: Evidence of early settlements
  • Religious Art: Antique wooden "Santos" from the Spanish era
