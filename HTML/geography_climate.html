I. The Geodetic Center of the Philippines

Marinduque holds a unique title as the home of the Luzon Datum of 1911, also known as Station Balanacan.

  • What is it? The primary survey point (the "0,0" coordinate) for all geological and topographic surveys in the Philippines.
  • Location: Mount Piit, Mogpog.
  • Why it matters: Every map of the Philippines is mathematically aligned to this exact point.
  • Coordinates: 13° 33' 45.43'' N, 121° 52' 04.23'' E

II. Physical Terrain

Marinduque is a volcanic island with a rugged mountainous interior and coastal plains.

  • Shape: Heart-shaped (cordiform)
  • Land Area: Approximately 952.58 sq km
  • Highest Point: Mount Malindig (1,157 meters)
  • Coastline: Towns are connected by a scenic circumferential road

III. Climate & Best Time to Visit

Marinduque experiences a Type III climate with no very pronounced rainy season.

  • Dry Season: March to May (peak tourism & Moriones Festival)
  • Cool Season: December to February (ideal for hiking)
  • Average Temperature: 26°C – 32°C

V. Flora and Fauna

Marinduque is a sanctuary of biodiversity both on land and underwater.

  • Butterfly Capital: Produces nearly 80% of the Philippines' butterfly exports.
  • Marine Life: Part of the Verde Island Passage, known as the "Center of the Center" of global marine biodiversity.
