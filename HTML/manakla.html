I. The "Crayfish" of Marinduque: Manakla

The most prized crustacean in Marinduque is not lobster, but the unique Manakla, a type of snapping shrimp found in mangroves and rice fields.

  • Habitat: Mangrove areas and rice paddies
  • Taste: Sweet like prawn, firm like crab
  • Signature Dish: Ginataang Manakla (cooked in coconut milk with ginger and chili)

II. Kari-Kari: The Island’s Dark Stew

Unlike the peanut-based Kare-Kare, Marinduque’s Kari-Kari is a bold and savory dish similar to dinuguan but richer and thicker.

  • Ingredients: Pig’s blood, internal organs, local spices
  • Texture: Thick and often served almost dry
  • Occasion: Common during fiestas in Santa Cruz and Torrijos

III. Adobo sa Ilong (Yellow Adobo)

This unique version of adobo replaces soy sauce with turmeric (dilaw), creating a bright yellow and aromatic dish.

  • Color: Vibrant yellow
  • Flavor: Garlicky, slightly earthy, and rich
  • Style: Oily and distinctly island-inspired
