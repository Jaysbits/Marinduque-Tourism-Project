I. The Caves of Marinduque – Into the Depths

Beyond its beaches, Marinduque hides a world of limestone caves filled with stalactites, underground rivers, and ancient legends. The towns of Santa Cruz and Boac offer unforgettable caving experiences for both beginners and thrill-seekers.

II. Bathala Caves (Santa Cruz)

The most famous cave complex, Bathala consists of eight caves, with four commonly explored.

Church Cave

  • Cathedral-like structure with a massive rock "altar"
  • Sunlight creates a glowing, heavenly atmosphere

Python Cave

  • Home to non-venomous pythons
  • Considered guardians by locals

Cemetery Cave

  • Contains ancient burial jars and artifacts
  • Shows pre-colonial burial traditions

III. Bagumbungan Cave (Santa Cruz)

A true adventure cave opened in the 2010s and strictly regulated.

  • The Experience: Waist-deep underground river trekking (1.9 km)
  • Formations: Crystal-like stalactites and stalagmites
  • Duration: 3–4 hours of crawling, climbing, and wading

IV. Cave Safety & 2026 Guidelines

  • Mandatory Guides: Entry only with licensed local guides
  • No-Touch Rule: Protect delicate cave formations
  • Gear Provided: Helmets and headlamps available

VI. Traveler’s Essentials

  • What to Wear: Dri-fit clothing, leggings/shorts, grip sandals
  • Best Time: December to May (dry season)
  • Don’t Forget: Dry bag for gadgets
