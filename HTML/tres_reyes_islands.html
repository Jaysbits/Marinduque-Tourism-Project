I. Tres Reyes Islands – The Royal Trio

Located off the coast of Gasan, the Tres Reyes Islands are a group of three islands named after the biblical Magi: Gaspar, Melchor, and Baltazar. They are among the most iconic maritime landmarks in western Marinduque.

II. Meet the Kings

Gaspar Island (The Largest)

  • Features: Has a small community and white coral sand beaches
  • History: Archaeological site with recovered Chinese porcelain artifacts

Melchor Island

  • Features: Rugged cliffs and rock formations
  • Vibe: Mostly uninhabited, ideal for scenic photography

Baltazar Island

  • Features: Known for diving spots and underwater wall
  • Landmark: Home of the Baltazar Lighthouse

III. Diving & Snorkeling (2026 Update)

  • The "Wall" Dive: Vertical reef drop with corals, tuna, and barracuda
  • Snorkeling: Gaspar Island offers coral gardens and sea turtles (pawikan)
  • Status: Designated Marine Protected Area (MPA)

V. Activities for Your Itinerary

  • Spelunking: Explore sea caves around Melchor Island
  • Picnicking: Enjoy meals on Gaspar Island huts
  • Photography: Capture the islands aligned at sunset

VI. Quick Facts

  • Water Clarity: Up to 20+ meters visibility
  • Entrance Fee: Small environmental fee required
  • What to Bring: Snorkel gear, sunblock, and water
