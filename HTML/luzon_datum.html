I. Luzon Datum of 1911 – The "Center of the Center"

Located at the summit of Mt. Mataas in Mogpog, the Luzon Datum of 1911 is the primary survey reference point of the Philippines. Established by the U.S. Coast and Geodetic Survey, it serves as the "0,0" coordinate for mapping the entire country.

  • Definition: A datum is a base reference point in surveying
  • Established: 1911
  • Marker: Stone and concrete base known as Station Balanacan

II. The Climb to the Top

  • Steps: Around 460 concrete steps to reach the summit
  • View: 360° view of Balanacan Port, Sibuyan Sea, and Mogpog hills
  • Religious Icon: Statue of Our Lady of Biglang Awa beside the marker

III. Why It Matters (The Science)

  • Coordinates: 13° 33' 45.430'' N, 121° 52' 04.230'' E
  • Purpose: Ensures maps across regions align correctly
  • Significance: Vital for national mapping before GPS technology
  • Centennial: Celebrated in 2011

IV. Visiting Tips (2026)

  • Best Time: Start climb at 6:30 AM
  • View Highlight: Balanacan Port’s calm, protected harbor
  • Nearby: Visit Paadjao Falls after the climb
