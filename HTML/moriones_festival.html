I. What is the Moriones Festival?

The Moriones Festival is a week-long Lenten celebration held annually during Holy Week (Maundy Thursday to Easter Sunday). It combines Catholic devotion, folk theater, and street spectacle.

  • The Name: Comes from "morion," meaning mask or helmet.
  • The Participants: Locals dress as Roman soldiers wearing colorful costumes and carved masks.

II. The Legend of Saint Longinus

The festival centers on Longinus, the Roman centurion who pierced Jesus Christ’s side.

  • The Miracle: His blindness was healed by Christ’s blood.
  • The Conversion: He became a Christian believer.
  • The Manhunt: Soldiers search for Longinus throughout the town.
  • The Pugutan: His execution reenacted on Easter Sunday.

III. The Mask: A Masterpiece of Folk Art

The Morion mask is both art and a sacred vow (Panata).

  • The Look: Fierce features carved from wood with decorative crests.
  • The Weight: Heavy and worn under intense heat as an act of sacrifice.

V. Quick Facts for Tourists

  • Where to watch: Boac, Gasan, and Mogpog
  • Respect: Many participants are fulfilling religious vows
  • The "Antipo": Playful Moriones who interact with the crowd
