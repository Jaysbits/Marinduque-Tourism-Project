I. The Putong (Tubong) – A Royal Welcome

The Putong (also known as Tubong) is a traditional Marinduqueño ceremony performed to welcome honored guests, celebrate birthdays, or mark special milestones.

In Marinduque, visitors are treated like royalty. During the ritual, performers—often women in traditional Filipiñana attire—sing, dance, and eventually crown the guest.

II. The Three Stages of the Ritual

The Greeting (Pag-akyat)

Performers gather at the entrance, singing to ask permission to enter and offer blessings.

The Praise (Puri)

The song becomes lively as they praise the guest and wish them health and long life.

The Crowning (Pagpuputong)

The climax of the ceremony, where performers shout "Viva!" while placing a crown on the guest and showering them with petals and coins.

III. Symbols of the Ceremony

  • The Crown (Corona): Symbol of honor and respect.
  • The Palm Leaf (Palaspas): Drives away bad spirits and brings peace.
  • Flower Petals & Coins: Represent blessings and prosperity.
  • Bamboo Sticks (Kalutang): Provide rhythm to the performance.

IV. Experience the "Viva!" (2026 Perspective)

  • Community-Led: Youth groups help preserve the tradition.
  • Tourist Favorite: Many resorts offer mini-Putong ceremonies for visitors.

Today, the Putong is not just a tradition—it is a living cultural experience that connects visitors to the heart of Marinduque.

V. Putong Lyrics (Sample Verse)

Nandito na kami, sa harap ng hagdan,
Humihingi ng pahintulot, sa may-ari ng bahay.
Ang aming pakay, ay mag-alay ng tuwa,
Sa panauhing mahal, na aming sinisinta.

(Translation: We are here at the foot of the stairs, asking permission from the homeowner. Our goal is to offer joy to the beloved guest we cherish.)
