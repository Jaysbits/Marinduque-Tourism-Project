I. Heritage Houses – Echoes of the Spanish Era

Marinduque is one of the few places in the Philippines where history is still lived daily. The towns of Boac and Gasan are filled with Bahay na Bato houses that have stood for centuries.

II. The Boac Heritage District

Walking through Mataas na Bayan feels like stepping back in time.

  • Architecture: Capiz shell windows, Narra floors, stone foundations
  • Casa de Don Emilio: Restored house turned restaurant and boutique hotel
  • Ventanillas: Small shutters for airflow from the river breeze

III. Gasan’s “Ancestral Row”

  • Artistic Flair: Vibrant pastel-colored houses
  • Balconies: Ornate ironwork used for relaxing and viewing the streets

IV. Inside a Bahay na Bato

Feature Purpose
Capiz Windows Let light in while reducing heat
Volada Gallery for servants to move around
Zaguan Ground floor storage for kalesas and harvest
Caida Main hallway for receiving guests

V. Preservation in 2026

  • Adopt-a-House: Restoration program by NHCP and local government
  • Adaptive Reuse: Homes turned into cafés, galleries, and homestays
  • Heritage Walk: Guided tours from Boac Plaza

VI. Quick Tips for Explorers

  • Be Respectful: Many houses are still private homes
  • Best Time: 7:00–9:00 AM for best lighting
  • Look Up: Check roof edges for intricate wood carvings (calado)
