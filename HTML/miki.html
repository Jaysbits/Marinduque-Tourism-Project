Tuba Miki Patis & Bagoong

I. Tuba: The Spirit of Marinduque

Coconut trees are considered the lifeblood of Marinduque, and Tuba is one of their most important products. It is a natural sap collected from the coconut bud.

  • Sweet Tuba: Fresh, sweet, and non-alcoholic when harvested early
  • Bahalina: Fermented tuba turned into a strong reddish wine
  • Tradition: Shared among locals as a symbol of community and bonding

II. Miki of Santa Cruz

Santa Cruz is known for its handmade Miki noodles, a staple comfort food in the province.

  • Production: Handmade in small family-run factories
  • Texture: Thick and chewy
  • Serving Style: Rich orange broth made from shrimp heads and annatto (atsuete)

III. Patis Dalahican

The fish sauce (patis) and shrimp paste (bagoong) used in Marinduque are known for their deep, rich flavor developed through traditional methods.

  • Process: Sun-cured fermentation lasting several months
  • Flavor: Strong umami taste
  • Use: Essential base for many vegetable and seafood dishes
