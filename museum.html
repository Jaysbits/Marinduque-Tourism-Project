Marinduque National Museum

Treasures of the Deep

Housed in a meticulously restored 19th-century Spanish building in Boac, this museum serves as the island’s cultural vault. Once a Presidencia (seat of government) and even a provincial jail, its thick stone walls now protect the island’s most precious artifacts.

II. The Shipwreck Gallery

Marinduque’s surrounding waters were vital nodes in the ancient "Silk Road of the Sea," a maritime trade route connecting the Philippines to the rest of Asia.

Gaspar Island Shipwreck

Discovered in the 1980s, this site yielded a massive collection of Ming Dynasty porcelain, evidence of early international trade in Marinduque.

Featured Artifacts
  • Celadon Plates
  • Blue-and-White Ceramics
  • Stoneware Storage Jars

III. Archaeological Wonders

Burial Jars

Artifacts from pre-colonial burial traditions found in the island's many limestone caves.

Stone Tools

Evidence of Marinduque's earliest settlements dating back thousands of years.

Religious Art

Antique wooden "Santos" and colonial relics from the Spanish era.
