The Putong (Tubong)

A Royal Welcome to the Heart of the Philippines

The Putong is a traditional Marinduqueño ceremony performed to welcome honored guests, celebrate birthdays, or mark special milestones.

In Marinduque, visitors are treated like royalty. During the ritual, performers—often women in traditional Filipiñana attire—sing, dance, and eventually crown the guest with floral headpieces.

II. The Three Stages of the Ritual

1. The Greeting (Pag-akyat)

Performers gather at the entrance of the house or venue, singing rhythmic verses to ask permission from the host to enter and offer their blessings.

2. The Praise (Puri)

Once inside, the tempo quickens. The song becomes lively as performers praise the guest’s character and virtues, wishing them divine health and long life.

3. The Crowning (Pagpuputong)

The climax! As the performers shout "Viva!", a crown is placed on the guest's head. They are showered with flower petals and coins while being fanned with palm leaves.

IV. Experience the "Viva!" (2026)

Community Preservation

In 2026, local youth groups in every municipality are leading the way in preserving the Putong, ensuring the ancient verses are never forgotten.

Interactive Tourism

Many resorts and homestays now include a mini-Putong ceremony for check-in guests, allowing you to feel the island's warmth immediately.

V. Putong Lyrics (Sample Verse)

"Nandito na kami, sa harap ng hagdan,
Humihingi ng pahintulot, sa may-ari ng bahay.
Ang aming pakay, ay mag-alay ng tuwa,
Sa panauhing mahal, na aming sinisinta."

Translation: We are here at the foot of the stairs, asking permission from the homeowner. Our goal is to offer joy to the beloved guest we cherish.
