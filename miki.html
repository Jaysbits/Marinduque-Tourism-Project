I. Tuba: The Spirit of Marinduque

Coconut trees are the lifeblood of the island, and Tuba is their most celebrated extract—a natural sap collected from the coconut bud.

Sweet Tuba

Freshly harvested, non-alcoholic, and naturally sugary.

Bahalina

Aged and fermented into a potent, reddish-colored wine.

Tradition: Tuba is often shared in a "tagayan" circle, symbolizing unity and community bonding.

🥥

100% Organic Sap

II. Miki of Santa Cruz

Handmade

Santa Cruz is the heart of Miki production, where noodles are crafted in family-run factories using time-honored techniques.

  • 🍜 Texture: Distinctly thick, chewy, and fresh.
  • 🥘 Serving: Served in a rich orange broth made from shrimp heads and annatto (atsuete).

III. Patis Dalahican

The island's Patis (fish sauce) and Bagoong (shrimp paste) are renowned for a deep umami flavor that can't be found in commercial brands.

The Secret:

A sun-cured fermentation process that lasts for several months in large earthen jars.
