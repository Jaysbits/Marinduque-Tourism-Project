I. Boac Cathedral – The Fortress of Faith

The Immaculate Conception Cathedral, also known as Boac Cathedral, is a historic "Fortress Church" built in 1792. Its massive stone walls were designed to protect locals from Moro pirate raids during the Spanish colonial period.

  • Architecture: Filipino-Spanish Baroque
  • Material: Adobe stone, coral, and brick with lime mixture

II. The Legend of “Biglang Awa”

The Virgen de Biglang Awa (Our Lady of Prompt Succor) is the beloved patroness of Marinduque.

  • The Siege: Townspeople hid inside during a pirate attack
  • The Apparition: The Virgin Mary appeared holding a glowing sword
  • The Victory: Pirates fled in fear of the divine vision
A footprint on the wall is believed to mark where she stood.

III. Architectural Highlights

  • The Retablo: Gold-leafed altar with detailed carvings
  • Bell Tower: Used as a lookout for incoming ships
  • Battlements: Castle-like roof for defense
  • Adobe Walls: Over five feet thick in some areas

IV. Role in the Moriones Festival

  • The Blessing: Masks are blessed on Holy Monday
  • Senakulo: Passion of Christ reenactments in the courtyard
